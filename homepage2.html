2. Caramoan Beach

Matukad Island is a small, uninhabited island located in the municipality of Caramoan, Camarines Sur, Philippines. This island is a popular destination for tourists because of its pristine white sand beach, crystal clear waters, and stunning rock formations. Visitors can hike up to the top of the island to see the famous Matukad Lagoon, a hidden natural pool surrounded by towering cliffs. Matukad Island is also home to a unique attraction – the Enchanted Milkfish, a species of fish that locals believe is guarded by a supernatural being. With its breathtaking views and magical allure, Matukad Island is truly a must-see destination in Camarines Sur.

Caramoan, Camarines Sur

3. Caloco Beach

Caloco Beach is a charming beach located in the town of Tinambac, Camarines Sur, Philippines. This long stretch of fine, white sand offers a picturesque view of the sea and the surrounding mountains. Caloco Beach is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and beach sports such as beach volleyball and frisbee. Visitors can also enjoy a variety of water activities such as snorkeling, diving, and fishing. Caloco Beach is an excellent destination for those who want to experience the beauty of a secluded and pristine beach. With its tranquil ambiance and stunning natural scenery, Caloco Beach is definitely worth a visit in Camarines Sur.

Caloco Beach, Tinambac, Camarines Sur

4. Siruma Beach

Siruma Beach is a 4th class municipality in the Province of Camarines Sur. Siruma Beach is a hidden gem that beckons to those seeking a tranquil escape amidst nature's beauty. Located in a secluded corner, this beach offers a serene retreat away from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. The soft, golden sands stretch for miles, inviting visitors to take leisurely strolls or simply unwind while listening to the soothing symphony of the waves.

Siruma, Camarines Sur

5. Hunongan Cove

Hunongan Cove is a secluded cove located in the municipality of Calabanga, Camarines Sur, Philippines. This hidden gem boasts of crystal clear waters and fine white sand, offering a serene and tranquil atmosphere for visitors. Hunongan Cove is ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing. Visitors can also explore the cove’s abundant marine life by snorkeling or diving. The cove is surrounded by lush greenery, providing a picturesque backdrop for relaxation and unwinding. With its stunning natural beauty and peaceful ambiance, Hunongan Cove is a must-visit destination for those who want to escape the crowds and immerse themselves in nature.

Siruma, Camarines Sur





Do's and Don'ts in Beaches

Do's:



  1. Dispose of trash properly: Carry a small trash bag with you and make sure to dispose of your garbage in designated bins. Avoid leaving any litter on the beach.
  2. Respect wildlife: Keep a safe distance from marine animals and birds. Avoid feeding them as it can disrupt their natural behavior and diet.
  3. Follow beach rules: Adhere to any posted rules and regulations, including those related to swimming, boating, and other activities.
  4. Use sunscreen: Protect yourself from the sun's harmful rays by using sunscreen. Choose reef-friendly options to minimize environmental impact.
  5. Stay on designated paths: Stick to marked trails and paths to protect dunes, vegetation, and nesting sites for wildlife.
  6. Be cautious with fires: If allowed, use designated fire pits or grills. Make sure to fully extinguish the fire before leaving the area.
  7. Respect other beachgoers: Keep noise levels to a minimum and be considerate of others enjoying the beach. Avoid playing loud music without headphones.
  8. Bring reusable items: Opt for reusable water bottles, containers, and bags to reduce the amount of single-use plastic on the beach.
  9. Educate yourself: Learn about the local ecosystem and any endangered species that may inhabit the area. This knowledge can guide your behavior to minimize impact.
  10. Practice water safety: Follow water safety guidelines, and if you're not a strong swimmer, consider using a life jacket when necessary.


Don'ts



  1. Littering: Never leave trash behind. Dispose of your waste properly to maintain the cleanliness of the beach.
  2. Disturbing wildlife: Avoid approaching or disturbing wildlife. Keep a safe distance and observe from afar.
  3. Feeding animals: Feeding wildlife can disrupt their natural behavior and diet. Resist the temptation to feed any animals you encounter.
  4. Walking on dunes: Stay off sand dunes to protect fragile ecosystems. Use designated paths and walkways.
  5. Unauthorized fires: Don't start fires unless there are designated fire pits or areas explicitly designated for that purpose.
  6. Ignoring safety guidelines: Pay attention to any safety guidelines and warnings, such as those related to strong currents or dangerous marine life.
  7. Excessive noise: Keep noise levels down and avoid playing loud music without headphones, especially in areas where it may disturb others.
  8. Using plastic products: Minimize the use of single-use plastic items. Opt for reusable alternatives whenever possible.
  9. Ignoring local rules: Familiarize yourself with and adhere to any posted rules and regulations specific to the beach you
  10. Ignore your health: Take breaks from the sun, stay hydrated, and be mindful of your limits.
